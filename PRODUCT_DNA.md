# GeoAware Product DNA

## Purpose
Product DNA defines what makes a GeoAware product itself. It protects identity without freezing implementation details.

Every GeoAware product must state four things:
1. **Identity** — What experience is this product uniquely responsible for?
2. **Non-negotiables** — What must remain true for the product to stay GeoAware?
3. **Inheritance from GeoAware-Core** — Which constitutional principles and ADRs does the product inherit?
4. **Drift boundaries** — What attractive ideas would make the product less itself?

## Required Product DNA Template

### Product Identity
Describe the product in one durable paragraph. The description should explain how the product deepens exploration, geography, Scripture, journey, language, worship, or guidance.

### Non-Negotiables
List the principles that cannot be traded away without changing the product’s identity.

### Inheritance From GeoAware-Core
Reference the constitutional articles and ADRs that the product inherits.

### Drift Boundaries
Name the behaviors, features, or market patterns that should be resisted unless the Project Architect explicitly approves a strategic change.

## Ecosystem Rules
- GeoAware products should make users feel like explorers rather than consumers.
- Maps are not decoration. Maps are the product.
- Geography is the interface.
- AI supports exploration. AI never replaces the product experience.
- Visible features must deepen exploration, geography, Scripture, journey, language, worship, or guidance.
- Implementation details may vary by product, but identity must remain consistent with GeoAware-Core.
