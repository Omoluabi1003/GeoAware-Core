# GeoAware Constitution v1.0

## Preamble
GeoAware exists to make people feel like explorers rather than consumers. The ecosystem is governed by a simple operating doctrine: **Think in YCOMBINATOR Mode. Execute in GeoAware 80/20 Mode.** This Constitution is the durable source of truth for product identity, decision memory, and institutional continuity across GeoAware products.

GeoAware-Core protects the identity of the ecosystem. It does not prescribe implementation details, framework choices, or application code unless such details are necessary to defend product identity.

## Article I — Purpose
1. GeoAware-Core is the canonical institutional memory and governance system for the GeoAware ecosystem.
2. Its purpose is to encode culture, product principles, decisions, operating doctrine, and review standards so future humans and AI agents inherit the same model.
3. GeoAware-Core is documentation-first. It should remain lightweight until repeated operational evidence proves that new tooling is necessary.

## Article II — Product Identity
1. Every GeoAware product must deepen exploration, geography, Scripture, journey, language, worship, or guidance.
2. Maps are not decoration. Maps are the product.
3. Geography is the interface.
4. Visible features must strengthen the user’s sense of place, motion, discovery, reverence, or orientation.
5. AI may support exploration, summarization, guidance, review, and workflow, but AI must never replace the product experience itself.

## Article III — Ecosystem Commitments
1. GeoAware Bible is a Geographic Bible Experience, not a generic Bible reader.
2. WaveAtlas is the canonical audio and radio engine for the GeoAware ecosystem.
3. Quiet Atlas exists to protect slow, reflective, low-noise exploration within the ecosystem.
4. Product identities must be explicit, inherited from GeoAware-Core, and protected from drift.

## Article IV — Authority
1. The Project Architect owns the vision, constitution, roadmap, product identity, and final approval.
2. AI collaborators may propose, draft, review, test, and summarize, but they do not own product authority.
3. Automated systems may enforce checks and surface risks, but they do not replace human judgment.
4. Final approval belongs to the Project Architect or an explicitly delegated human authority.

## Article V — Operating Doctrine
1. GeoAware thinks in YCOMBINATOR Mode: clarify the customer, isolate the wedge, compress scope, prefer evidence, and protect momentum.
2. GeoAware executes in GeoAware 80/20 Mode: ship the smallest durable change that advances the mission, avoid premature tooling, and preserve optionality.
3. Documentation, decision records, and product DNA are first-class work products.
4. The repository should encode the operating model so that contributors can act consistently without relying on private context.

## Article VI — Review and Change Management
1. Changes must protect product identity before optimizing implementation detail.
2. Non-blocking reviewer nits must be bundled into meaningful PRs unless they are blockers.
3. A blocker is a concern that materially affects correctness, safety, product identity, governance, user trust, or delivery risk.
4. Review should distinguish constitutional issues from implementation preferences.
5. PRs should be coherent, reviewable, and tied to durable intent.

## Article VII — Decision Memory
1. Architectural Decision Records in `decision-records/` preserve the reasons behind ecosystem-defining choices.
2. ADRs should state status, context, decision, consequences, and review triggers.
3. Decisions may evolve, but they must not be silently erased.
4. Superseded decisions should remain readable and link to their replacement.

## Article VIII — Product DNA
1. Every product must define its identity, non-negotiables, inheritance from GeoAware-Core, and drift boundaries.
2. Product DNA constrains feature selection, reviews, and AI-generated proposals.
3. A feature that cannot explain how it deepens exploration, geography, Scripture, journey, language, worship, or guidance should not be treated as a GeoAware feature.

## Article IX — Amendment Process
1. Amendments require an explicit proposal that states the problem, affected articles, intended outcome, and risks.
2. Amendments must be reviewed as governance changes, not routine copy edits.
3. The Project Architect has final approval over constitutional amendments.
4. Accepted amendments must update the version history below and, when appropriate, add or update an ADR.
5. Minor clarifications may be made without changing the major version when they do not alter authority, product identity, or operating doctrine.
